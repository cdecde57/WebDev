B1 The CPU

What is The CPU

Where do you begin when learning about computers? Well, it all starts with what you have in front of you! You have a keyboard, a mouse, a screen, and then a computer. All of these items work together to help you perform daily tasks with your computer. Although I would love to walk through and explain everything from the top to the bottom, I cannot do that here since for my school assignment I don't have the time. Let us begin our journey with understanding your computer itself, and the foundations behind how it works.

Your computer has a bunch of pieces within it! These things help control your computer. Some of these components include the 'RAM' chips, your 'Mother Board', your 'Hard Drive', the 'Power Supply Unit' and the 'CPU'. We will focus our time here on the 'CPU', The Central Processing Unit. this is the brain of your computer. Everytime you drag your mouse or click a key on your keyboard, an electric signal goes to your computer's CPU to instruct it to do something such as 'type the letter A' or 'move my mouse to the top of the screen'.


The other parts of your computer help the CPU. For instance the 'Power Supply Unit' or 'PSU' gives power to the machine which is critical to turning on and using the computer. The 'Hard Drive' you have in your machine holds all of your files. The CPU will draw upon the data here to write or save different files. The RAM chips within your computer are known as 'Random Access Memory' they hold memory or data that is used by the CPU. Your CPU will work with these chips a lot when computing and performing tasks. Anytime you open a program on your computer, information about it is stored in the RAM so the CPU can compute and display the application (with the help of your computer screen and a graphics processing unit - essentially a 'cpu' for your monitor) and let you use its' functionalities. The 'Mother Board' is a big 'ol chip in your computer that everything connects to. The motherboard is the glue that connects everyone to each other.


Are you ready to explore how the CPU controls everything on your computer? Well thankfully the idea is very simple. You know how I mentioned that one thing, the 'Power Supply Unit' and how it powers your computer? Well, it allows your computer to turn on, yes, but electricity is also how all the other chips and pieces will communicate with each other! It is how the CPU will compute data and communicate with your monitor to show applications on your screen! So how does this happen? How does electricity have this 'power' in communication? Well, just like we humans talk, computers talk too... Not English or Spanish, but something called binary! I will teach you this language in 4 words - ready? - "Power On Power Off". You can now speak fluent binary! Congratulations! Well, I guess more accurately you know the alphabet so-to-speak for binary. Lets try to better understand what Binary really is!
